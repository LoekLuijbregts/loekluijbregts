The Story

Thirty years silent.
Two years reborn.

On Pravia's central square stands a building that once embodied the grandeur of the Indiano era — merchants returned from the Americas who poured their fortunes into palatial homes. For three decades, ours stood empty, its 34 hand-carved wooden windows sealed, its 400 m² of ornate plasterwork slowly fading behind scaffolding and locked doors.

Then Fleur and Carlos fell in love with it. What followed was not a renovation. It was a resurrection — every floorboard relaid, every wall stripped and re-plastered, the original roof re-tiled, each window restored by craftsmen who still knew how. All of it done slowly, deliberately, in the spirit the building deserved.

The result is a home. And now, yours to stay in.

Meet Fleur & Carlos

Fleur grew up in the Netherlands and spent years managing island retreats and luxury restaurants before following Carlos — and her heart — to Asturias. Carlos is from here. He knows the fish market vendor by name, the best sidrería table to grab on a rainy Tuesday, and exactly which trail through the chestnut forest takes you to the waterfall nobody else finds.

Together they spent two years rebuilding this house with their own hands and a small team of local craftsmen. They live next door. Breakfast is made fresh every morning. They will always have a recommendation for you.

The Region

Green Spain at your door

Thirty minutes can take you from a mountain pass to an empty Atlantic beach. We'll help you plan every day — or do absolutely nothing at all.

≈ 90 min drive
Picos de Europa

Spain's most dramatic national park — limestone giants with the legendary Ruta del Cares carved into the gorge. The Lagos de Covadonga glacial lakes. Take the day. Take two.

25 – 40 min drive
Atlantic Beaches & Surf

Asturias has 200+ beaches — wild, wide, rarely crowded. Salinas for surf, Rodiles for legendary barrels, Playa de San Juan for golden sand. All on the same coastline.

In town & 15 min
Cider, Fabada & Sidrerías

Asturian cuisine is one of Spain's best-kept secrets. Fabada with morcilla, fresh Nalón salmon, Cabrales cheese, and cider poured from height. We know the real places.

Passes through Pravia
Camino del Norte

The northern Camino runs through this landscape — mountains on one side, the Cantabrian Sea on the other. Walk a stage, or commit to a week. Casita Azul is the perfect base.

Walking distance
Pre-Romanesque Heritage

The Iglesia de Santianes is one of Spain's oldest Christian churches. The Route of the Indianos reveals extraordinary architecture from the returning Americas emigrants. History, no crowds.

From the door
Cycling, Kayaking & Rivers

The Nalón and Narcea rivers are legendary for salmon and kayaking. Cycle routes wind through chestnut forests and apple orchards. We keep bikes. We'll arrange the rest.

How We Do Things

Small by design.
Intentional by nature.

We are not a hotel. We are a home that happens to have three rooms. Everything here — the breakfast, the recommendations, the conversation over cider — comes from people who genuinely love this place.

🌿

Sustainability

Local builders, regional materials, seasonal food. Breakfast comes from the Thursday market fifty metres away. Nothing packaged, nothing shipped far.

🏠

Heritage

Every restored detail — the plasterwork, the windows, the tilework — honours the craftspeople who built this house 130 years ago. Nothing was replaced if it could be saved.

🤝

Community

We send guests to local restaurants, local guides, local shops. Tourism that puts money back into Pravia and the families who've always been here.

🐢

Slow Travel

Two nights minimum — not because we have to, but because Asturias rewards those who stay. Give it three days and it will change you. Give it a week and you won't want to leave.
