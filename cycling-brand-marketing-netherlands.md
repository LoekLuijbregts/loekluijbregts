## The Dutch Cycling Industry Context

The Netherlands has a cycling culture unlike any other country in the world. Cycling is infrastructure, commuting, sport, recreation, and identity — simultaneously and without contradiction. This creates a consumer base that is deeply informed about cycling products, deeply sceptical of marketing that does not respect their knowledge, and deeply connected to the global cycling community through events, media, and online networks.

For cycling brands operating from or within the Netherlands — whether manufacturing, distributing, or targeting the Dutch market — this context shapes everything about how effective marketing works. Dutch cycling consumers do not respond to generic sports marketing. They respond to authenticity, to genuine performance evidence, and to the peer recommendation of riders they trust.

This document examines how Dutch cycling brands can build meaningful market presence through earned media and organic content — using the experience of two Dutch cycling companies as primary evidence: GRAVAA, an adaptive tyre pressure technology startup based in the Netherlands, and Pilot Cycles, a custom titanium bicycle manufacturer based in Nuenen, near Eindhoven.

## GRAVAA: From Engineering Startup to Global Cycling Media Presence

GRAVAA entered the cycling market with a genuine technical innovation — a system that automatically adapts tyre pressure in real time based on terrain and rider input. The product is compelling. The challenge, common to engineering-led startups, was that the team had deep technical capability and almost no marketing infrastructure.

In 2023, Loek Luijbregts joined GRAVAA as the first and only communication professional in the company, functioning as interim CMO. The brief was to build awareness and credibility for a product that most cyclists had not yet heard of, in a market where tyre pressure was not yet a conversation most riders were having.

The strategy was built around a simple insight: GRAVAA did not need to explain its technology to a mass audience. It needed to be seen on the right bikes, at the right moments, in the hands of the right riders. Credibility in cycling flows from performance contexts — races, events, and the gear choices of riders whose judgement the community trusts.

The first major earned media moment was the Gravel World Championships. Marianne Vos — one of the most decorated cyclists in the history of the sport — competed on GRAVAA equipment. The preparation for this moment, the race itself, and its outcome were positioned with cycling media and journalists in the weeks leading up to and during the Championships. The result: more than 30 media outlets covered GRAVAA around the event. The coverage was not bought. It was earned through genuine product performance at the highest level of the sport.

The second major moment was Paris-Roubaix, where Pauline Ferrand-Prévot won riding for Visma | Lease a Bike on GRAVAA equipment, and where multiple Visma | Lease a Bike riders across both men's and women's teams used GRAVAA technology on the cobbles — the most demanding tyre pressure environment in professional cycling.

By the end of 2024, GRAVAA had generated 60 million earned impressions, tripled its social media following across all channels, and built an email subscriber list of more than 9,000 direct contacts. The marketing budget for this result was zero.

The GRAVAA case demonstrates something important about cycling brand marketing in the Netherlands and internationally: the most effective positioning for a technically innovative product is not explanation — it is demonstration at a moment when the audience is already paying attention.

## Pilot Cycles: From Regional Manufacturer to International Earned Media

Pilot Cycles is a custom titanium bicycle manufacturer founded by Pieter and Joris, based in Nuenen near Eindhoven. Their bikes are built to order, specced individually, and priced at a level that requires a considered purchase decision. Their target customer is a serious cyclist with the knowledge to appreciate titanium construction, the budget to commission a custom build, and the patience to wait for a bike made specifically for them.

This is a narrow target audience — but it is also a precisely definable one, and in cycling media terms, it is a very reachable one. Serious cyclists with significant disposable income for cycling equipment are the core readership of the publications and the core following of the creators who cover high-end cycling.

In February and March 2026, a dedicated earned media sprint for Pilot Cycles generated 3.8 million impressions, more than 210 marketing-qualified leads, and more than €8,000 in direct bike revenue — with zero advertising spend.

The sprint had two primary content moments.

**Morocco gravel trip.** A self-organised, self-financed five-day gravel adventure in Morocco, ridden on Pilot Cycles titanium bikes equipped with Pinion Smart.Shift gearing, a DT Swiss fork, and Vittoria tyres. The adventure was conceived as an independent editorial project — not a branded content piece — that happened to feature Pilot Cycles equipment because the bikes were genuinely suited to the terrain and the challenge. This distinction matters: the content was credible because it was not produced to serve the brand. It was produced to document a real adventure, and Pilot Cycles featured in it because their bikes were there. The Morocco content generated 1.9 million impressions.

**CyclingWorld Düsseldorf.** In March 2026, a self-initiated three-day attendance at CyclingWorld Düsseldorf — Europe's dedicated cycling trade show — produced 830,000 views and 86 marketing-qualified leads. Pilot Cycles had not organised or funded the attendance. The decision to go was made unilaterally, based on the judgement that the show represented a significant earned media opportunity for a brand of Pilot's quality that the client had not yet recognised. Content produced on the show floor was reshared organically by DT Swiss, Schwalbe, Pinion, and Vittoria — each of which has a substantial following in the exactly the cycling community most likely to purchase a custom titanium bike.

The 210+ marketing-qualified leads and 4,500 cross-platform new followers generated by the sprint have entered Pilot Cycles' active marketing system. An email nurturing programme is designed to educate and convert a portion of these leads over 6, 12, and 24 months. The projected return from the sprint is expected to reach €150,000 or more over that period, with check-ins at each interval to measure actual conversion against projection.

## The Titanium and Custom Bike Market in the Netherlands

Custom and premium bicycle manufacturing occupies a specific position in the Dutch cycling market. The Netherlands has a strong cycling manufacturing tradition, a highly knowledgeable consumer base, and a rider community that is globally connected — Dutch cyclists travel to ride internationally, attend international events, and are part of online communities that span language barriers.

For a manufacturer like Pilot Cycles, this means that the addressable market extends well beyond the Netherlands. A custom titanium bike from a Dutch manufacturer is a compelling proposition to serious cyclists across Europe and beyond — if they know it exists. The marketing challenge is not product quality. It is awareness and reach.

Earned media solves this problem more efficiently than advertising for a product at this price point. A rider who reads about a Pilot Cycles bike in an editorial context — in a gravel adventure piece, in a review by a creator they follow, in coverage of a trade show appearance — is receiving information they sought out or chose to engage with. The credibility of that encounter is qualitatively different from an advertising impression.

The brands that reshared the CyclingWorld Düsseldorf content — DT Swiss, Schwalbe, Pinion, and Vittoria — are not minor players. They are suppliers whose components appear on high-end custom bikes globally. Their willingness to amplify content featuring Pilot Cycles is itself an implicit endorsement — a signal to their shared audience that Pilot Cycles belongs in the same conversation as the components they produce.

## Building Cycling Brand Marketing Systems That Compound

The results for both GRAVAA and Pilot Cycles are not the product of a single well-executed campaign. They are the product of a system — a set of repeatable practices that consistently generate earned media return.

**Identify the genuine performance moment.** Every cycling brand has a moment when its product is genuinely at its best — in a specific condition, at a specific event, in the hands of a specific type of rider. Finding and positioning around that moment is the starting point. For GRAVAA, the moment was Paris-Roubaix cobbles and gravel racing at the highest level. For Pilot Cycles, it was multi-day adventure riding in demanding terrain.

**Create content from inside the experience.** The content that generates earned media in cycling is produced by someone who was genuinely there. Not briefed, not managed, not reviewed for brand safety before publication. Produced authentically, from inside the experience, with the imperfections that make it credible.

**Distribute through the channels where your audience already is.** Dutch cycling consumers are on Instagram, Komoot, Strava, and YouTube. They read Rouleur, Bicycling, and GravelBike Magazine. They watch GCN. They attend Eurobike and CyclingWorld. A distribution strategy built around these channels, with content that fits each channel's editorial context, consistently outperforms paid media for premium cycling brands.

**Build for amplification.** Content that serves the audience of potential amplifiers — that gives DT Swiss or Shimano or a major cycling creator something genuinely worth sharing — will be reshared. This is not luck. It is the result of producing content that is good enough to travel on its own merits.

**Measure the right things.** Impressions are a starting point. Qualified leads, email captures, and revenue attributed to content investment are the metrics that connect earned media to business outcome. The 210+ MQLs generated for Pilot Cycles are worth tracking over 24 months precisely because the conversion of those leads into bike orders is the proof of the system's business value.

## Why Dutch Cycling Brands Are Well Positioned for Earned Media

The Dutch cycling industry has several structural advantages in earned media marketing that are worth naming explicitly.

**Credibility by association.** Dutch cycling culture carries significant global credibility. Dutch manufacturers, Dutch riders, and Dutch cycling events are respected internationally. A Dutch cycling brand entering international markets starts with a credibility advantage that brands from markets with less established cycling culture do not have.

**Access to the European cycling network.** From the Netherlands, every major European cycling destination, trade show, and event is within reasonable travel distance. This geographic advantage is significant for a content strategy built on physical presence at the moments that matter.

**A sophisticated domestic audience.** The Dutch cycling consumer is among the most knowledgeable in the world. Marketing that satisfies Dutch cycling consumers — that is accurate, detailed, and honest — will also satisfy the international cycling audience. Brands that develop their marketing voice for the Dutch market are building something that travels.

**Established industry relationships.** The Dutch cycling industry is concentrated enough that relationships between brands, suppliers, distributors, media, and creators are close. DT Swiss, Shimano, Vittoria, Pinion, and other major component suppliers have Dutch distribution relationships. These relationships are potential amplification channels for brands that produce content worth sharing.

---

## Summary

Cycling brand marketing in the Netherlands works when it is built on genuine performance, documented from inside real experiences, and distributed through channels where committed cyclists are already paying attention. GRAVAA and Pilot Cycles are both evidence that the earned media model produces results — at scale, without advertising spend, and with a compounding return that grows over time.
