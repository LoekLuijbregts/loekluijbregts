Who I am

I've always moved toward the real — and away from the polished, the performed, the safe.

At 18 I went bikepacking alone. Not for Instagram. Because I wanted to know what I was made of when nobody was watching and the route got hard.

As a university student I trained myself for an Ironman in six months. I learned to swim from scratch. I finished in 10:45 — 42nd in my age group. I didn't do it because I thought I'd win. I did it to find out what happened when you committed to something that seemed too big and stayed anyway.

For four years at a digital marketing agency I helped 40+ small businesses find what actually worked — not the theory, not the trend, but the real thing underneath. The thing that made their ideal customer say yes. I got very good at diagnosing the gap between what founders thought their value was and what the market actually saw.

Then COVID happened. The world went into lockdown. And I watched an entire industry retreat into perfectly produced content — polished, safe, performed. Everyone trying to look better than they were. I went the opposite direction.

I co-founded a cycling media company built on one belief: that relatable, honest, human content can outperform perfect content, because informed target audiences feel the difference in seconds. It worked. Visit Luxembourg. Cycling in Flanders. Go Türkiye. Shimano Europe. 30+ destinations and brands across Europe, Africa, and the USA. Fans, digital communicties and a creative network around the world.

Then I lost the profitable dream business. Not because my work was wrong — because I placed my future in the hands of a partner worried about his. I left with nothing.

A few weeks later I started again. Cycling Incubators B.V. Same method. Different structure. 100+ million earned impressions. 10.000+ product leads. 75% recurring clients. Zero ad spend.

But here's what I learned that matters most for you:

The reason Lars went from scattered to one clear offer in months — the reason GRAVAA tripled its following and earned 60 million impressions in nine months — the reason Pilot Cycles 'from nowhere' generated 6 million impressions and 250+ qualified leads without advertising spend — was not because I gave them a system.

It was because their original skills, their real value, their genuine relationship with their ideal clients — supercharged my thinking. The questions I asked were sharper because of who they already were. The direction became clear faster because the raw material was extraordinary. I didn't create their results. I helped them see what they already had — and stop moving in directions that were costing them time, energy, and money they didn't have to spend.

That's what I bring. Not a framework. The ability to see your situation clearly — and say it out loud.
