Atlas Mountains,
Morocco
1,200 km over 12 days
22,000 m elevation gain
Gravel & piste
2024
Campaign origin

Two weeks self-supported through Morocco's High Atlas — loaded with a kit that had to double as camera gear, camping equipment, and two weeks of nutrition. The conditions ranged from 40°C tarmac to snow on the Tizi n'Test pass. Every frame shot on an iPhone from the handlebar bag or a roadside rock. The content from this expedition generated more than 8 million organic impressions and placed in three major cycling publications before the trip was even finished.

This route is now used by two cycling tourism operators as a reference itinerary. The destination marketing started because people kept asking where we rode.

Paris–Roubaix
Media Ride
257 km
29 sectors of cobbles
Road & pave
2024
Case study

Riding Paris–Roubaix as a media attachment for GRAVAA — their electronic derailleur mounted on the bike, the camera rolling from inside the experience rather than from the press truck. The difference between shooting a product and riding a product across cobblestones at race pace is the entire argument for rider-first content. No studio shot has the same credibility as a system that kept working after 257 km of Arenberg.

The resulting content earned placement in Rouleur, GCN, and Eurosport. Total earned reach from one race weekend: 31 million impressions.

Competing at Gravel Worlds as both rider and content creator — the only way to produce footage that actually looks like what it feels like to race. The cobbled classics are the annual calibration — a reminder of what cycling looks like when difficulty is the point and suffering is the spectacle. Riding Flanders' bergs and sectors informs everything from brand voice to content pacing. There's a rhythm to how cycling's hardest races build narrative tension. That rhythm shows up in campaign structure when you've ridden the routes yourself.

Home terrain and the daily baseline — the Netherlands' cycling infrastructure is the world's most sophisticated and the most misunderstood by outsiders who reduce it to flat Amsterdam streets. The route network through Brabant, Limburg's climbs, and the gravel tracks of the national forests makes up the weekly training ground. Most of the destination marketing work for Dutch cycling tourism started with routes that were already mapped in the legs before they were mapped on paper.

Rwanda & Kenya
3 weeks
Rwanda, Kenya
Road & dirt
Campaign origin

Riding in East Africa changes the scale of what cycling means. In Rwanda, the national team trains on the same mountain roads that serve as daily commuting routes. In Kenya, cycling is infrastructure before it is sport. The content from this trip became the case for adventure cycling marketing beyond Europe — a market that most European cycling brands have not yet reached and that has enormous latent demand.
